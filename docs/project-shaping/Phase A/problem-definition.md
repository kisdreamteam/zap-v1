## 1. Product Identity

We are **not** building a general AI story generator.

We are solving a classroom engagement and English-practice problem for ESL/EFL teachers working with beginner students ages 6–8. The product — **Zap!** — is a **teacher-controlled, AI-assisted classroom storytelling activity** designed to support **active classroom participation**, not passive entertainment.

---

## 2. Target User

### Primary user (V1)

**ESL / EFL teachers**

### Student participants (not direct users)

Students ages **6–8**, beginner / early learners (**A1–A2**), participate **through the teacher**. They do not have accounts in V1.

Student constraints that shape the problem:

* short sentences and simple vocabulary
* simple choices with clear cause/effect
* concrete story events
* light humor
* limited abstract reasoning
* classroom-friendly content

### Not V1 users

* Students with accounts
* Parents
* Novel writers
* General consumers
* Creative writing hobbyists
* Entertainment users

---

## 3. Classroom Context

The activity is designed for a **typical school class (15–30 students)**.

It runs mainly through a **projected classroom screen**: the teacher controls the app, and the whole class watches together. This context means the experience must work as a **whole-class, teacher-led activity** — not a solo or small-group tool.

---

## 4. Problem Statement

ESL teachers need a **low-preparation, occasional classroom activity** (~30–40 minutes) that helps beginner students generate ideas, express ideas in English, stay engaged, think creatively, discuss options, and collaborate.

The real competition is **not another app**. It is **boredom, passive learning, repetitive activities, and highly stimulating entertainment** ("dopamine thieves") that make traditional classroom activities feel flat by comparison.

---

## 5. Why This Matters

Teachers are competing with highly stimulating media and entertainment. To hold a class's attention, an educational activity must feel:

* fun
* immediate
* collaborative
* surprising
* easy to run

…while still being educational. Without that balance, young learners disengage — especially in a whole-class setting where participation must be managed across 15–30 students.

---

## 6. Current Alternatives

Teachers may currently use:

* story prompts
* discussion games
* story cubes/cards
* writing exercises
* ChatGPT manually
* teacher-created activities
* classroom games

---

## 7. Why Existing Solutions Are Insufficient

Current alternatives are often:

* repetitive
* prep-heavy
* hard to adapt live in the moment
* overly dependent on teacher creativity and energy
* less engaging for young beginner learners

None of these reliably delivers a **low-prep, whole-class, collaborative English activity** that produces a **shared artifact the class feels ownership over**.

---

## 8. Why Storytelling Specifically

Collaborative story-building creates **shared ownership** — the class feels they **made something together**.

That collective artifact is the differentiator. Compared to roleplay games, debate games, mystery games, escape room activities, or speaking games, storytelling gives the whole class a **visible, co-created result** they can revisit and take pride in. The story is not just an exercise; it is **theirs**.

Storytelling is the vehicle; shared ownership is the reason it works for this problem.

---

## 9. Desired Outcome

A teacher can **launch an activity quickly** and run a **30–40 minute special classroom session** that students enjoy and actively participate in.

This is **not** imagined as a daily curriculum platform. It is an **occasional special classroom activity** — something teachers reach for when they want a change of pace that still supports English practice.

Measurable success, failure, and validation criteria are defined in [success-criteria.md](success-criteria.md). Product boundaries are defined in [product-boundaries.md](product-boundaries.md).

---

## 10. One-Sentence Problem Definition

> ESL teachers of beginner students (ages 6–8) struggle to run engaging, low-prep classroom activities that get the whole class participating in English — storytelling is the vehicle because collaborative story-building gives the class shared ownership of something they created together.
